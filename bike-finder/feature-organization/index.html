Feature Organization

From all the personas we have collected, I prioritized user stories that should be on the website.


-As an online shopper, she wants to use an online shopping website that makes her want to buy a bike so that she doesn’t have to leave her comfort home.
-As someone who hates online shopping, I want to be able to quickly find the bike I want with as few clicks as possible so that I don’t lose it trying to navigate all these pages and buttons.

-> Necessary components for convenience: Checkbox (Filter in list page), Buttons (Cart, Add to cart, Add to wishlist), Search bar (Auto-suggestion feature), Personal Information, FAQ, Sorting(dropdown), Color Options of the bike, Similar products, Price and discounted information


-As a first time bike buyer, I want help knowing which type of bikes are available so that I can make an informed purchase.

-> Quick test to help them learn about bike so that they can find a proper bike


-As someone who is a bit disgruntled about the process of buying a bike, I want to easily know which bikes are most suited to my purpose.

-> Checkbox: Filter in list page


-As someone who enjoys online shopping, I want to see a detailed photo of a bike without visiting a store.

-> Large photo of the bike zoom in feature, and rotation feature


-As someone who doesn’t know much about bikes, I want to know others’ opinions on the bike so that I can make a good judgment about which one to buy without doing copious amounts of research.
-As an experienced shopper, I want a detailed and thorough site with reliable reviews so that I can feel comfortable purchasing a bike without riding it.

-> Detailed customer reviews (and stars)


-As someone who is on a budget, I want to be able to easily identify bikes that are on sale or discount.

-> Top sales menu (Bikes that are on sale or discount)


-As a parent purchasing a bike for my child, I want to see what other bike accessories I should get with the bike so my child can be safe.

-> Customers who viewed this item also viewed


-As an impatient, irritable person, I want to know EXACTLY what sorts of is on my bike so that I don’t have to look up more information and spend more time on the internet. If I wanted a lecture I would’ve graduated from college.
-As someone who will be taking supplies with me on my trips, I want to know the weight limit of the bike.

-> Detailed spec of bikes


-As a first time bike buyer, I want to be able to compare a bike I am interested in to other bikes on the website in order to determine the best fit for my child.
-As someone who owns multiple bikes, I want access to lots of specs so that I can compare to the bikes that I already have or have had.

-> Showing similar product : Do you want to compare the specs? (It will have access to lots of specs so that they can also compare to the bikes that already have or have had)


-As someone who goes off roading, I want the option to have a warranty or access to servicing.
-As someone who knows very little about bikes, I want to know how I can get my bike serviced so that I know who to call if anything breaks or doesn’t work. Also, I don’t want to do any manual labor besides biking itself which I don’t really want to do but my doctor insists if I want to keep both of my feet.

-> Warranty information (Explanation about the warranty how it works), FAQ


-As someone who has specific needs and is knowledgeable about bikes, I want to know if and how I can customize a bike to my needs.

->Showing small parts that can be bought together: Do you want to customize?


Therefore, I have summarized the things that I want to have on my bike website as follows.

Main Page

  • Brand title (Brand icon)
  • Cart button (They can easily know what they already put in the cart and how many put in the cart)
  • Wishlist button
  • Top sales (Trending)
  • Search bar (with auto-suggest feature)
  • FAQ
  • (Left half) Bike picture with a sentence on top saying “Don’t know what you want to get?” Check out to find out! (Button) (To them learn about bikes so that they can find a proper bike : This will be a short test to know how much they know about the product, and the users will learn about the bike from the right answers of the test. The test will include ~10 basic questions about the bike (bike types, bike wheels, bike materials, bike brands and so on) and it will be multiple choice questions. Users will have to select one answer from 4 different choices. After submitting their answer each question, it will tell them the right answer with explanation of basic knowledge about the bike.
  • (Right half) Bike picture with a sentence on top saying, you already know what you want to get? Check out our products! (Button) -> This will lead to a list page.

List page for all products

In this list page, it will show all of the products we have in the store. Users will be able to filter the bike that they are looking for. They will also be able to enter their age and height to get the better result.

  • Personal information
    • Height
    • Weight
    • Age
  • Checkbox: Filter (on the left corner)
    • Price
    • Material
    • Color
    • Speed
    • Gender
    • Purpose
    • Wheel size
    • Frame size
    • Rating
    • Model
    • Bike Weight
    • Brand
    • Type of bike
  • Dropdown: Sorting (at the top of list of products)
    • Price:low-high
    • Price:high-low
    • New arrival
    • Top customer review
  • List of products (This will show bike image, name, brand, price, ratings, discount)

Detailed Product Page

Once they click the product from the list page, it will lead them to the product page for the details. It will show them followings

  • Large photo of the bike
    • Zoom in feature and rotation feature
  • Name of the bike
  • Brand of the bike
  • Warranty information
  • Detailed spec of the bike
    • Size of the bike wheel and bike body
    • Type of the bike
    • Materials
    • Weight limit of the bike
  • Color options
  • Showing small parts that can be bought together (on the right corner)
    • Button : Do you want to customize?
  • Showing similar products (on the right corner)
    • Button : Do you want to compare the specs? (It has access to lots of specs so that they can also compare to the bikes that already have or have had)
  • Customers who viewed this item also viewed
  • Price and discounted price with shipping cost included
  • Short description of the bike
  • Customer reviews (stars)
  • Add to cart button
  • Add to wishlist button
